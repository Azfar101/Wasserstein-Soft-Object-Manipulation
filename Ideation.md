# Fundamental Contributions for Unsupervised Skill Discovery over Deformable/Granular Media: A Prior-Art Map and Ranked Framing Analysis

## TL;DR
- The strongest fundamental, general, and tractable contribution is **"skill discovery whose diversity signal is a physical optimal-transport (Wasserstein/EMD) metric on the external material state"** — a metric-aware skill objective in the METRA/LSD lineage whose ground metric is EMD-on-mass over the soil/dough/cloth field, and whose discriminator is conditioned only on that external state. An independent novelty check found no paper combining all four ingredients; each exists separately.
- A second, mathematically deeper framing — **skills as controlled invariants / stabilizable directions in the material's state space (Lyapunov/contraction certificates + Benamou–Brenier transport)** — is the strongest theory claim but harder to build; it is best positioned as the certificate layer that upgrades transport-diversity from "diverse" to "certifiably steerable," giving the goal-reaching layer a principled backbone rather than serving as the headline.
- Recommendation: build **Transport-Metric Skill Discovery (TMSD)** as the core contribution — a domain-general objective that discovers skills covering the Wasserstein manifold of material configurations, instantiated first on excavator-soil (differentiable MPM sim) and generalized to a second deformable domain (2D dough or cloth). Add a lightweight Wasserstein-geodesic skill-sequencing layer for goal-reaching as the demonstration, and a contraction/Lyapunov certificate as the "genuine math underneath." Target CoRL/RSS for the empirical paper, with a NeurIPS/ICLR theory companion possible.

## Key Findings

**1. The unsupervised skill-discovery landscape is mature but has a clean, specific hole.** The field has moved from mutual-information (MI) objectives — DIAYN (Eysenbach et al., 2019), DADS (Sharma et al., 2020), VIC, CIC — to *metric-aware / distance-maximizing* objectives that fix MI's blindness to state-space geometry. LSD (Park et al., 2022) maximizes Euclidean traveled distance under a Lipschitz constraint; CSD (Park et al., ICML 2023) uses a controllability/density metric; METRA (Park et al., ICLR 2024) replaces MI with the Wasserstein Dependency Measure (WDM). Per the METRA paper, "we propose to use the temporal distance … between two states as a distance metric d_temp(s1,s2), the minimum number of environment steps to reach s2 from s1," and the key conceptual point is that "MI is completely agnostic to the underlying distance metric, while WDM is a metric-aware quantity." Critically, METRA/DSD define a general template — "WDM with any ground metric d(·,·)" — but every instantiated d is either learned-latent (temporal) or Euclidean. **No one has instantiated d as a physical optimal-transport metric on a material's mass field.**

**2. Object-/external-state-conditioned intrinsic reward exists — but only with MI, never with transport.** MUSIC (Zhao, Gao, Abbeel, Tresp, Xu; ICLR 2021 Spotlight, arXiv:2103.08107) states: "We mathematically formalize this reward as the mutual information between the agent state and the surrounding state under the current agent policy … able to complete the pick-and-place task for the first time without using any task reward." This is exactly the "discriminator on external state only" structure the researcher proposes, but the signal is MI/KL. DUSDi (Hu et al., NeurIPS 2024) and SkiLD (Wang et al., NeurIPS 2024) condition on factored/object state and reward inducing distinct factor interactions — again MI-based. The swap "MUSIC-style external-state conditioning + Wasserstein/EMD diversity" is unclaimed.

**3. Skill discovery has essentially not been applied to deformable/granular/fluid material.** Skill discovery on manipulation is confined to rigid objects. Meanwhile deformable-object learning heavily uses EMD/Wasserstein — but always as a *goal-matching reward or evaluation metric*, never as a reward-free diversity signal. DiffSkill (Lin, Huang, Li, Tenenbaum, Held, Gan; ICLR 2022, arXiv:2203.17275) learns skill abstraction from a differentiable physics simulator, then "plan[s] over the skills by finding the intermediate goals" on dough tasks (LiftSpread, GatherTransport, CutRearrange). RoboCraft (Shi et al., 2022; GNN + Chamfer/EMD losses), RoboCook (Shi et al., 2023), PASTA, and "Make a Donut" (hierarchical EMD-space planning) all optimize toward a target dough shape. This is the second half of the gap.

**4. The mathematical scaffolding the researcher hoped for is real and mostly unclaimed in this combination.**
- *Dynamical optimal transport (Benamou–Brenier)* recasts W₂ as the minimum kinetic energy of a density evolving under a continuity equation — literally "steering the continuity equation with minimal energy," a control problem. Elamvazhuthi (arXiv:2407.16088, 2024, LANL/UC Riverside) proved that "the results also establish controllability of the continuity equation whenever the corresponding Kantorovich problem admits a feasible solution, leveraging the equivalence between the Kantorovich and Benamou-Brenier formulations," for nonlinear control-affine systems on ℝⁿ. This is the rigorous bridge between "transport" and "control" that makes skills-as-transport-maps well-founded.
- *Wasserstein-over-Wasserstein (WoW)* — OT whose ground cost is itself W₂ between distributions — is now formally developed ("Flowing Datasets with Wasserstein over Wasserstein Gradient Flows," 2025) but has never been applied to RL/control. The soil surface is naturally a field/measure, and a distribution *over* skill-conditioned material configurations is a distribution-of-distributions — a natural WoW object.
- *Control-contraction metrics / control-Lyapunov functions* (Manchester & Slotine, 2017) turn "can this direction be stabilized?" into pointwise LMIs and supply the certificate machinery for "skills as controllable directions."
- *Granular-surface PDE models* (Prigozhin critical-slope variational model; BCRE) describe the soil surface height u(x,t) with the gradient constraint |∇u| ≤ tan α (angle of repose). Crucially, per Barrett & Prigozhin (ESAIM:M2AN, 2007): "Solutions to Monge-Kantorovich equations, expressing optimality condition in mass transportation problem with cost equal to distance, are stationary points of a critical-slope model for sand surface evolution." This is a striking, citable link grounding "soil shaping = optimal transport under a slope constraint."
- *Shape-space geometry* (LDDMM/diffeomorphometry, Kendall shape space) provides a metric on surfaces-as-deformations; recent robotics work already uses diffeomorphic latent shape spaces (e.g., latent-diffeomorphic end-effector co-design), showing the machinery ports to robotics.

**5. Simulation tooling is ready for a single graduate student.** Genesis (unified differentiable MPM/SPH/FEM/PBD engine, 2024–2026) supports granular *and* elasto-plastic *and* cloth *and* fluid materials in one API with a differentiable MPM solver — so the excavator-soil case AND the dough/cloth generality experiment can be built in one framework. DDBot (2025) already demonstrates differentiable MPM soil digging; TERA and the Egli/Hutter et al. line show RL excavation is an active, credible sim domain.

## Details

### 5.1 Honest prior-art map

**Skill-discovery objectives (chronological, with what each fixes):**
- **MI family** — DIAYN maximizes I(S;Z)=H(Z)−H(Z|S) via a discriminator q(z|s); reward log q(z|s) − log p(z). DADS adds transition dynamics; CIC uses contrastive state-entropy. Known failure mode, stated verbatim by the CSD authors (Park et al., ICML 2023): "previous approaches (LSD and DIAYN) only learn to gain control of the agent's own 'body' (i.e., the gripper and joint angles), completely ignoring the object in the FetchPush environment." This is directly relevant: naïve MI skill discovery will move the *arm*, not the *soil*.
- **Distance-maximizing family (DSD)** — LSD (Euclidean, Lipschitz), CSD (controllability/density), METRA (WDM + learned temporal distance). METRA is SOTA and scales to pixels. Its own stated limitation: temporal distance is embedded into a *symmetric* Euclidean latent though temporal distance can be asymmetric.
- **MISL re-unification** — "Can a MISL Fly?" (2024) shows METRA's gains can largely be re-derived inside MI skill learning (contrastive successor features, CSF), so "MI vs. Wasserstein" is a live, contested framing — useful context, and a reviewer will raise it.
- **Object/factored variants** — MUSIC (external-state MI), DUSDi (disentangled per-factor MI), SkiLD (factor-interaction graphs). These are the closest "discriminator-on-external-state" precedents, all MI-based.
- **Wasserstein-diversity variants** — WURL (He, Jiang, Zhang, Shao, Ji; AAAI 2022, arXiv:2110.07940): "we propose a new framework Wasserstein unsupervised reinforcement learning (WURL) where we directly maximize the distance of state distributions induced by different policies … amortizing the overall reward to each step." WIC (Durugkar et al., 2021) maximizes Wasserstein from the start state using a time-step metric. Both use generic/Euclidean/time-step ground metrics on the full agent state — not EMD on a material field, not external-state-only.

**Deformable/granular manipulation learning:**
- Representations in use: point clouds + GNNs (RoboCraft, RoboCook), latent spaces (DiffSkill/PASTA via PointFlow), heightmaps (granular shaping RL; "Interactive Shaping of Granular Media," 2025), particles (MPM). EMD/Chamfer are the standard shape-distance losses.
- Excavation RL: soil-adaptive digging (Egli et al., 2022), bucket-filling (Egli et al., 2024), TERA sim (2025), DDBot differentiable MPM digging (2025), rock/boulder capture (2025). All are task-reward-driven; none discover skills unsupervised.
- Sequencing over skills: DiffSkill/PASTA plan intermediate subgoals in latent/EMD space; RoboCook uses human-defined subgoals + GNN planning; "Make a Donut" plans in EMD space zero-shot. This is exactly the sequencing layer to borrow for the demonstration.

**Verdict on the two candidates the researcher pre-identified:**
- *Object-centric transport as diversity signal* — **survives and is the strongest core**. The novelty is specifically: (a) an EMD/Wasserstein *physical* ground metric on the material mass field, plugged into (b) a DSD/WDM metric-aware skill objective, with (c) the representation/discriminator conditioned *only* on the external material state. This four-way combination is genuinely unclaimed.
- *Skills-as-controlled-invariants (Lyapunov/contraction)* — **survives as the theory/certificate layer, not the headline**. On its own it risks being read as "PDE boundary control + RL wrapper," an application of existing control theory. Its highest-value use is to certify that discovered transport-directions are *stabilizable*, converting diversity into steerability and underwriting goal-reaching.

### 5.2 The recommended contribution: Transport-Metric Skill Discovery (TMSD)

**One-sentence claim.** *We introduce a general principle for unsupervised skill discovery over external material/field states: reward skills for spreading apart the skill-conditioned distributions of the material configuration under a physical optimal-transport metric, and show that the resulting skills form a coverage of the Wasserstein manifold of material states that composes into goal-reaching.*

**Why it is fundamental and general (not an application).** The unit of novelty is the *diversity signal itself* — replacing MI/temporal/Euclidean diversity with an OT metric whose ground cost is the physical cost of rearranging material mass. This is domain-independent: it applies to any agent manipulating an external state that is a measure or field (soil heightfield, dough mass, cloth mass, fluid). The excavator-soil case is the first instantiation, not the point.

**Objective (concrete).** Represent the material state at time t as a measure μ_z,t on the workspace (soil mass per unit area, i.e., a normalized heightfield or particle mass distribution). Let φ be conditioned only on the external state. Following the WDM/DSD template, maximize a Wasserstein Dependency Measure between the (external) material state and skill z, with the ground metric

  d(s, s′) = W₂(μ_s, μ_s′)  (2-Wasserstein / EMD between mass distributions),

optionally under a critical-slope-aware cost (Prigozhin) so that transport respecting the angle of repose is "cheaper." Concretely, per-step intrinsic reward can take the LSD-style directional form r = (φ(s′) − φ(s))·z under the Lipschitz constraint ‖φ(x) − φ(y)‖ ≤ W₂(μ_x, μ_y), so latent displacement is upper-bounded by *physical transport cost*, forcing skills to differ by how they move soil mass. Because the discriminator sees only the soil field, skills that merely reconfigure the arm earn no reward — directly solving the DIAYN/LSD "completely ignoring the object" failure mode documented by the CSD authors.

**Representation choice (resolve the researcher's tension).** Do NOT make representation the contribution. Use a **spectral/operator-agnostic** front end so the objective is representation-portable: the heightfield as an element of a function space, with a truncated basis (2D Fourier / Chebyshev for a bounded excavation bed; the Fourier-neural-operator literature shows band-limited spectral layers are resolution-independent on uniform grids). The Wasserstein cost is computed on the mass distribution (entropic/Sinkhorn OT for differentiability, or sliced-Wasserstein for speed). Report an ablation over representations (spectral vs. neural-latent vs. raw heightmap-EMD) to show the *objective* — not the representation — carries the result. This keeps generality and avoids being "tangled in one representation."

**Algorithm outline.**
1. Simulate excavator + soil in Genesis (differentiable MPM) or Taichi/DEM.
2. Encode soil surface → mass measure μ (spectral or particle representation); robot proprioception kept out of φ.
3. Skill-conditioned policy π(a|s,z); train φ (Lipschitz w.r.t. the W₂ ground metric) + policy with the transport-metric WDM/DSD reward, using SAC/PPO.
4. Sinkhorn or sliced-W₂ for the OT computation; amortize with a critic (as WURL does) to combat reward sparsity.
5. (Demonstration layer) For goal-reaching to a target soil state μ*, plan a sequence of skills as a *discrete geodesic on the Wasserstein manifold*: receding-horizon selection of z minimizing residual W₂(μ_current, μ*), i.e., skill-space planning as in DiffSkill/PASTA but with the transport metric native to the objective.

**The "genuine math underneath" (certificate layer).** Invoke Benamou–Brenier: a skill that moves μ along a W₂ geodesic realizes minimum-kinetic-energy transport, i.e., steering the continuity equation. Elamvazhuthi's (2024) result gives conditions under which such steering is achievable by control-affine dynamics — "controllability of the continuity equation whenever the corresponding Kantorovich problem admits a feasible solution." A contraction/CLF certificate on the reduced (spectral) coordinates certifies that a selected skill *decreases* W₂ to the local target — upgrading "diverse skills" to "certifiably steerable skills" and giving the sequencing layer a Lyapunov-style convergence argument. This is the part that makes the paper more than empirical.

### 5.3 Ranked candidate framings

| Rank | Framing | Novelty | Generality | Tractability | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Transport-metric skill discovery** (EMD ground metric + external-state discriminator, DSD/WDM lineage) | High (unclaimed combo) | High (any material field) | High (Genesis + Sinkhorn) | **Core contribution** |
| 2 | **Skills as controlled invariants / Wasserstein geodesic flows** (Benamou–Brenier + contraction certificates) | High theory | High | Medium–Low | **Certificate/theory layer of #1** |
| 3 | **Wasserstein-over-Wasserstein diversity** (distribution *of* skill-conditioned material distributions) | Very high, never in RL | High | Low–Medium (nested OT costly) | High-risk stretch goal / theory section |
| 4 | **Koopman-operator skills** (skills as eigen-directions of the material's transfer operator) | Medium | Medium | Medium | Weaker; Koopman-RL already exists |
| 5 | **PDE-boundary-control skills** (Prigozhin sandpile as controlled PDE) | Low–Medium | Low (granular-specific) | Medium | Best as *cost-shaping* inside #1, not standalone |

Framing #3 (WoW) is the boldest: the object of diversity is a *distribution over material fields*, and its ground cost is W₂ between fields — a two-level OT that is mathematically novel in RL and maps exactly onto "distribution of distributions." Include it as a theoretical generalization/section rather than the empirical spine, because nested OT is computationally heavy.

### 5.4 Empirical plan and metrics
- **Primary domain:** excavator arm shaping soil in MPM (Genesis). Skills learned reward-free.
- **Generality domain (required):** 2D dough shaping (PlasticineLab-style) *and/or* cloth — reuse the same objective unchanged to prove domain-independence.
- **Skill-diversity metrics:** coverage of the W₂ manifold (spread of terminal μ_z), pairwise W₂ between skill-conditioned state distributions, state-coverage vs. DIAYN/LSD/METRA baselines.
- **Goal-reaching metrics:** final EMD/W₂ to target μ*, success rate at threshold, number of skills to reach goal, vs. (a) task-reward RL from scratch, (b) DiffSkill-style latent planning, (c) goal-conditioned METRA.
- **Ablations:** representation (spectral vs latent vs raw), ground metric (W₂ vs Euclidean vs temporal — i.e., is the transport metric doing the work?), external-state-only vs full-state discriminator (does excluding proprioception matter?).
- **Certificate check:** empirically verify monotone W₂ decrease under the sequenced skills (contraction evidence).

### 5.5 Publication positioning
- **Primary venue:** CoRL or RSS (robot-learning audience values the deformable-manipulation instantiation + generality). ICRA/IROS as fallback.
- **Theory companion / alternative:** NeurIPS or ICLR if the WDM-with-physical-ground-metric analysis + Benamou–Brenier controllability + WoW generalization are developed formally.
- **Claim structure:** (1) principle (transport-metric diversity on external field states), (2) instantiation (excavator-soil), (3) generality (dough/cloth), (4) certificate (steerability → goal-reaching).
- **What reviewers will attack, and defenses:**
  - *"This is just METRA/LSD with a different metric."* → The metric is not a detail: it is a *physical, non-learned* ground cost that (a) fixes the object-ignoring failure mode via external-state conditioning, and (b) makes skills correspond to transport directions with a controllability interpretation. Show the ablation where Euclidean/temporal metrics fail to move soil meaningfully.
  - *"MI can do this too" (the MISL/CSF critique).* → Concede the theoretical near-equivalence for latent metrics; argue the *physical* OT ground metric is what MI-based methods lack and cannot easily incorporate, and demonstrate empirically.
  - *"Representation is doing the work."* → Pre-empt with the representation ablation.
  - *"EMD is expensive / reward is sparse."* → Sinkhorn + sliced-W₂ + WURL-style amortized stepwise rewards.
  - *"Generality is asserted, not shown."* → The second domain (dough/cloth) is mandatory, not optional.

## Recommendations
1. **Commit to TMSD as the headline** (framing #1). Write the claim as a general principle for skill discovery over external material/field states; make excavator-soil the first of ≥2 instantiations.
2. **Build in Genesis** (differentiable MPM, one engine for soil + dough + cloth). Prototype the objective first on a 1-D/2-D toy heightfield to debug the Sinkhorn reward before full 3-D soil.
3. **Freeze the representation question by ablation, not commitment.** Ship a spectral (Fourier/Chebyshev) front end as default for portability; include neural-latent and raw-heightmap-EMD as ablations. This directly answers the "don't get tangled in representation" requirement.
4. **Add the certificate layer** (contraction/Lyapunov monotone-W₂-decrease + a Benamou–Brenier framing paragraph) to secure the "real math" bar and to justify the goal-reaching sequencer. Keep it as one theorem + empirical monotonicity plot, not the whole paper.
5. **Sequencing = demonstration, via Wasserstein-manifold geodesic planning** (receding-horizon skill selection minimizing residual W₂ to μ*). Borrow DiffSkill/PASTA planning machinery.
6. **Stage the WoW generalization (framing #3) as a theory section / future work** — high novelty, high cost; include the reduced single-level EMD ground metric in experiments, reserve nested OT for analysis.
7. **Benchmarks/thresholds that change the plan:** If the transport-metric ablation does NOT beat Euclidean/temporal metrics on soil-moving coverage, pivot the headline to the *external-state-only + certificate* angle (skills-as-controllable-invariants). If Genesis MPM is too slow for RL throughput, drop to a 2-D DEM/heightfield surrogate for the main experiments and use MPM only for a validation domain.

## Caveats
- The novelty verdict rests on the absence of a specific four-way combination; a very recent (2025–2026) workshop or preprint could pre-empt it. A final targeted arXiv scan of cs.RO/cs.LG for "skill discovery deformable Wasserstein" is advised before submission.
- The "MI vs. Wasserstein" debate is unsettled ("Can a MISL Fly?" argues METRA's benefits fit inside MI skill learning); the paper must argue the *physical* ground metric — not Wasserstein per se — is the load-bearing novelty, and show it empirically.
- WURL's exact ground-metric implementation (Euclidean vs. learned critic over raw state) should be verified against the AAAI PDF, since it is the closest "Wasserstein diversity" precedent and the distinction is load-bearing for the novelty claim. (Note: the WURL arXiv identifier is 2110.07940; do not confuse it with WIC, arXiv:2110.15331.)
- Some engine/tooling claims (Genesis differentiability scope, solver coverage) come from project documentation and should be validated by a small throughput benchmark before committing the full experimental design.
- Controllability results (Elamvazhuthi 2024) are for control-affine systems on ℝⁿ; extending the certificate rigorously to the excavator's true (contact-rich, non-smooth) dynamics is nontrivial — present it as a principled motivation + reduced-coordinate certificate, not a full closed-loop guarantee on the real system.
- EMD/Wasserstein on a *conserved-mass* soil field is well-posed; on domains where material enters/leaves (excavation removing soil, pouring) use *unbalanced* OT — flag this modeling choice explicitly. The Prigozhin critical-slope constraint |∇u| ≤ tan α should be encoded into the ground cost so that discovered skills and planned transports respect the angle of repose.
